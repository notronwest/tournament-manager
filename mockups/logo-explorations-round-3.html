03, 04, 09, 10 — three takes each

The four you liked, with three variations apiece. Brand frame shifted to Tournament Management specifically — so where there's room (pennant + letterpress), "TOURNAMENT MANAGEMENT" lives in the lockup as a tagline. Hand-painted and swing-wordmark stay tighter — they're identity, not signage.

Each row holds one direction's three takes. Some show on a cream / yellow / red stage so you can see how the mark sits against the colors it'll actually live on.

Direction 09

Wordmark + paddle-swing — three takes

The original chunky Russo wordmark with a hand-drawn swing line underneath. Variations: refine the original (9A), swap to a condensed face (9B), or trade the wavy line for a hard angled slash that reads as the swing locked in (9C).

9A — Refined Russo

Chunky lowercase, cleaner S-curve

Original direction with the swing arc redrawn — steadier sweep, no kink in the middle. Russo One stays the wordmark; red ampersand carries the personality.

BERT&ERNE
9B — Condensed Anton

Tall caps, sport-ticker proportions

Same swing under different type. Anton's tall condensed caps push toward tournament-banner energy — pairs better with a print poster than the lowercase original.

Single angled bar, finish locked in
