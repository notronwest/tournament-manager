Mockup — shopping-cart registration flow

Replace the current "click Register → atomic confirm" flow with a cart-based one where picks accumulate, partners are picked at checkout, and nothing writes to event_registrations until payment lands. Stack of screens below shows the full path with notes calling out design decisions and open questions.

Anchoring decisions baked into this mockup. Push back on any of these.
  • Single-tournament cart. One tournament at a time. Cross-tournament adds Stripe Connect complications (each org has its own Connect account). Starting a second tournament's cart prompts to clear or finish the current one.
  • Cart lives in localStorage until checkout. Survives sign-out, refresh, browser close. Re-validated against current event capacity at checkout time.
  • Partners picked at checkout, not at add-to-cart. Lower friction to add; partner decision happens in the review step alongside everything else.
  • Invites + emails fire after payment. No partner notification until the money is committed.

Capacity re-validation at checkout. Because nothing is written to the DB while the cart sits in localStorage, an event can fill up between add-to-cart and checkout. Re-check capacity on the checkout page load + again right before the Stripe redirect. If something filled, drop it from the cart with a red banner explaining what happened and offer the waitlist (when waitlists ship). Note also that the "first event" classification can shift when items get dropped — the surviving Mixed 3.5-3.99 is now the first event at $60 instead of an additional at $20.
Bigger open questions for the design discussion
  • Does the existing edit-mode register page survive? Today's /register lets a registered player change partners or withdraw. Under the cart model, those mutations on an EXISTING registration probably don't go through the cart at all — they stay as direct edits (same flow as today). The cart is for net-new picks only.
  • What about free tournaments? If total is $0, do we still route through Stripe? Probably bypass it and just write the rows directly. Stripe Checkout requires > $0.
  • Abandoned-cart visibility (organizer side). Storing the cart in localStorage means we can't see it from the server. To enable the "organizer sees who's browsing" story, we'd need either (a) a lightweight cart_drafts table written on each cart change, or (b) a "save my picks" button that writes a draft, or (c) accept that visibility ends at "they hit Pay."
  • Partner-change side effects under cart model. Same logic as commit C in the recent PR — cancel old invite + email + new invite — but it now runs at payment-webhook time, not at the moment the user clicks confirm. Slightly different ordering implication: if the user re-edits before paying, the old invite hasn't fired yet.
  • Refund semantics. Withdrawals from a cart-based registration land squarely in the existing withdraw/refund-flow item in the backlog — nothing new here, but worth checking that the same code path handles cart-originated regs cleanly.
